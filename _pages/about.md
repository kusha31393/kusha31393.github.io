I am **Kusha Sridhar**, a Sr. Manager at the [Accenture Center for Advanced AI](https://www.accenture.com/us-en/services/data-ai) in Mountain View, CA, USA. My work sits at the intersection of **Speech AI** and **Agentic systems** — I build production voice agents that converse in real time, understand emotion, and operate under the tight latency budgets that real applications demand.

Before Accenture, I was a Staff Research Scientist at [Hippocratic AI](https://www.hippocraticai.com/), where I led streaming real-time TTS (4.16 MOS at sub-200ms latency) and speech-to-speech assistants for healthcare, plus production speaker-diarization (<120ms under 200+ concurrent requests). Earlier, at Sony Interactive Entertainment's Future Technology Group, I led the emotional voice synthesis team that automated 95% of PlayStation's voice production pipeline and shipped AI features — voice cloning, de-aging, speech editing — into AAA titles, resulting in three US patents.

I earned my **PhD in Electrical Engineering from The University of Texas at Dallas** in 2021, advised by [Prof. Carlos Busso](https://carlosbusso.com/) at the Multimodal Signal Processing Lab. My dissertation, *Unsupervised Personalization and Deep Uncertainty Modeling for Speech Emotion Recognition*, looked at how speaker-dependent traits (particularly in valence) and predictive uncertainty can be leveraged to make emotion recognition robust in the wild. I was also the lead researcher behind the [MSP-Podcast Corpus](https://www.lab-msp.com/MSP/MSP-Podcast.html) — to date the largest naturalistic speech emotion dataset. I then spent a year as a Postdoctoral Research Associate at Rice University in affective computing, working on physiological sensing for clinical intervention in opioid craving. I hold an MS in Electrical Engineering from the University of Southern California (2017) and a BE in Electronics and Communication Engineering from PES University, Bangalore (2015).

## Research interests

My work focuses on interpretable neural architectures for human-centered machine intelligence. Current focus areas:

- **Full-duplex conversational voice AI** — back-channeling, mid-response cancellation, barge-in logic, Active Echo Cancellation via adversarial gradient flow
- **Speech LLMs and speech-to-speech models** — generative TTS, voice cloning, de-aging, speech editing, emotional control of generated voice
- **ASR pipelines at scale** — low-latency ASR with granular diarization and word-level timestamps for downstream agentic workflows
- **Voice Agentic AI** — voice-first agents that reason, plan, and act across multi-turn dialog; tool use over speech; orchestration of ASR, LLM, and TTS under real-time latency budgets; interruption-aware dialog policies that stay natural under barge-in and partial ASR
- **Multimodal Agentic AI** — Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models for computer use (GUI navigation, desktop automation, long-horizon agents over screens and structured tools), and spatial/visual RAG to improve retrieval by building layout-aware data structures and enhancing it with multimodal embeddings at the retrieval stage

I review regularly for **IEEE TAFFC**, **IEEE/ACM TASLP**, **ICASSP**, **Interspeech**, **SLT**, and **ACII**.
