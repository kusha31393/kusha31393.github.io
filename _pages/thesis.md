## Unsupervised Personalization and Deep Uncertainty Modeling for Speech Emotion Recognition

**Kusha Sridhar**
*Ph.D. Dissertation, The University of Texas at Dallas, 2021*
*Advisor: [Prof. Carlos Busso](https://carlosbusso.com/), Multimodal Signal Processing Lab*

## Overview

My dissertation asks a question that had received surprisingly little attention in the speech-emotion-recognition (SER) literature: *how do we make emotion recognition systems actually reliable — across speakers, across recording conditions, and in the face of the label noise that naturalistic emotion data always carries?* The answer, I argue, requires two complementary ideas: **unsupervised personalization** to close the gap between training speakers and target speakers, and **deep uncertainty modeling** to let the system abstain when it has no business committing to a prediction.

## The MSP-Podcast Corpus

Throughout the PhD I was also the lead researcher on the [MSP-Podcast Corpus](https://www.lab-msp.com/MSP/MSP-Podcast.html) — to date the largest naturalistic speech emotion dataset, with 400+ hours of emotionally rich spontaneous speech and a quarter-million+ annotated speaking turns. I designed and implemented the full pipeline: podcast mining, speaker diarization, cleaning, emotion retrieval with DNN frameworks, and crowd-sourced annotation on Amazon Mechanical Turk. Eight versions of the corpus have been released to academic and industrial partners. A full corpus paper (Busso et al., arXiv:2509.09791, 2025) is now available.
